Still Here

A story about surviving yourself

Some films are built on budgets. This one was built on something far more lasting — love, courage, and the quiet urgency of needing to tell the truth. Created with nothing but a beating heart and the will to share it, Still Here is a raw and intimate short film that follows a young LGBT boy as he navigates the wreckage left behind by the cruelty of others.

He carries wounds he didn't ask for — the kind carved by people who should have known better. Desperate to be free, he tries to scrub every bad memory from his mind, to erase the past the way you erase ink from a page. But some stains go deeper than skin. The weight of it all pulls him toward the darkest edge of himself, until he stands at the threshold of wanting to disappear entirely.

And then — something shifts.
Not a miracle. Not a grand rescue.
Just a quiet, trembling realization that this is not where his story ends.

That life — messy, painful, unfinished life — is still asking something of him. That learning to accept the scars is not defeat. It is the bravest thing a person can do.

Still Here does not ask you to forget the hurt. It asks you to stay anyway.
